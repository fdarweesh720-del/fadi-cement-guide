Standard Overview

Scope

This standard covers 27 distinct common cements, 7 sulfate resisting common cements, and 3 low early strength blast furnace cements.

Publication Date

September 2011

Cement Types

5 main types: Portland, Portland Composite, Blast Furnace, Pozzolanic, and Composite Cements

Key Features

  • Comprehensive composition requirements
  • Performance criteria specifications
  • Quality control measures
  • Environmental considerations

Cement Types

CEM I - Portland Cement

95-100% clinker

Pure Portland cement with at least 95% clinker content. Highest early strength development, suitable for general construction.

Applications:
  • Structural concrete
  • High-strength applications
  • General construction

CEM II - Portland Composite

65-94% clinker

Portland cement with various additives (slag, fly ash, limestone, etc.). Good durability, reduced heat of hydration.

Applications:
  • General construction
  • Marine structures
  • Mass concrete

CEM III - Blast Furnace

5-64% clinker

Cement with significant blast furnace slag content. Low heat of hydration, high sulfate resistance, slow early strength.

Applications:
  • Mass concrete
  • Marine environments
  • Sulfate-aggressive conditions

CEM IV - Pozzolanic

45-89% clinker

Cement with pozzolanic materials (natural pozzolana, fly ash). Good long-term strength, reduced permeability.

Applications:
  • Durable structures
  • Chemical resistance applications

CEM V - Composite

20-64% clinker

Cement with combination of slag and pozzolanic materials. Very low heat of hydration, excellent durability.

Applications:
  • Mass concrete
  • Marine structures
  • Long-term durability
